Splitting

A blunted knife will not kill you. It will only hurt sweetly. Slipping the edge on your skin will not cause splitting. Maybe if you press hard enough, long enough, Want enough.

If there is any splitting that splitting will be slow splitting and, bruised splitting and, to be frank, hard to keep splitting, before shaking apart. Not the quick lighting-like slitting of a sharpened knife. Splitting or slitting with a sharp or blunted knife. These are different. These are different and they Should be different but both will hurt pleasantly and unpleasantly but a dull knife will do oh so dully but we would rather live to feel the splitting than die swiftly by the slitting and we think that’s rather right.

One could argue the outcome is the same. A fresh gap in flesh. But a slitting is a slipping and a splitting is a ripping. These fit different scars. A slitting is cleaner and fleeter and neater but slipping is always deeper. A splitting looks worse than it is; ripping’s always a mess.

But, really, a splitting isn't a ripping and neither a slitting is a slipping. A ripping requires gripping and a slipping is an accident. A slitting has never been an accident. A splitting might be, but there is much more pressure to be sure and so it’s usually not. A slitting is a slitting and a splitting is a splitting and neither of them are a ripping or a slipping or an accident.
