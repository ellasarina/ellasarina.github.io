Varmits

You’re a cowboy of some variety, holed up with your crew in an abandoned cabin deep in the Rockies. It’s about midnight. Y'all were woken up by a suspect noise and go to investigate. Find out if its a Varmit or a Beast.

2-6 players, 1 player will be a varmit or beast (that player can either choose or shuffle the tokens and pick randomly. Do not share with the rest of the players). The V/B is in charge of the Clue Cards and will hold them all in their hand.

Phase 1: Investigatin’

Collect clues throughout the house and yard

  • The player on the Left of the V/B goes first, and it continues clockwise.
  • The player rolls 1d6 to determine how many squares they can move. If they enter a new room on their turn, they can choose to roll 1d6 to see what they see. The V/B Chooses a card in their hand that corresponds with the Location and number on the die (Ideally, they will choose cards that will accurately reflect their identity, but it is only required for rolls of 4 or higher). The Cowboy adds the clue card to their hand, and may choose whether or not to share it with the rest of the crew.
  • This continues until a Cowboy has a pretty good idea of which V/B it is. At that point, they have a choice to Shoot or Shoo.

Phase 2: Shoot or Shoo

Make a choice, it will have consequences.

  • If the Cowboy Shoos a Varmit, the Varmit will have the choice to fight or flee, depending on their Wiles (stats). Next up is Phase 3.
  • If the Cowboy Shoos a Beast, the Beast will attack and kill that cowboy. They may choose to fight or flee afterwards, but the first contact is fatal. Move on to Phase 3.
  • If the Cowboy Shoots a Varmit, the Varmit will die and the game will end.
  • If the Cowboy Shoots a Beast, the Beast’s Blood (HP) will go down by 1 and Phase 3 will begin.

Phase 3: Fight or Flee

Varmits and Beasts have different stats, but they can both Fight or Flee.

  • The V/B places their Player Token on The Roof and begins their turn immediately. They may use one (1) of their Wiles to instigate an action (See the end to get the V/B’s specific Wiles)
  • Using Chomp: This is your attack. The V/B may attack a cowboy in one of the adjacent squares. You will make a contested roll, each adding your attack bonus. If the V/B wins, the Cowboy takes full damage, if the V/B loses, the Cowboy takes half damage, rounding up.
  • Using Scramble: This is your movement. You may move through any open space (unless specified otherwise in Wiles)
  • Using Charm: You may attempt to Charm your opponents. To do this, you must make a contested roll with a Cowboy your V/B could reasonably see. Each player rolls 1d6 and adds their charm bonus. If the
  • Using a Wild Action: These can only be used once per game, so use them wisely! You can activate them after a normal action (unless specified otherwise in Wiles).

  • The Cowboys now use their turn to instigate an action based on their Wiles.
  • Using Shoot: This is your attack. A Cowboy may attack the V/B up to two (2) squares away. You will make a contested roll, each adding your attack bonus. If the Cowboy wins, the V/B takes full damage, if the Cowboy loses, the V/B takes half damage, rounding up.

Wiles:

  • Possum
  • Chomp- 1
  • Scramble- 2 (possums may fit through ground floor windows)
  • Charm- 2
  • Blood- 3
  • Wild Action! The Possum may Hide in the Wall once per game. This regains all their Blood and they may move up or down one (1) level in the house while remaining in the same square. They are invulnerable for the rest of the turn (because they’re in the walls).

  • Wild Action! The Werewolf may Howl at the Moon once per game. This forces all Cowboys to move away from the Werewolf three (3) squares
  • Vampire
  • Chomp- 2
  • Scramble- 4 (vampires may fit through attic windows)
  • Charm- 2
  • Blood- 6
  • Wild Action! The Vampire may Suck ‘em Dry once per game. If the Vampire kills a Cowboy, they may choose to regain the amount of Blood equal to their Chomp damage.
  • Cowboy
  • Shoot: 1
  • Scramble: 2
  • Charm: 1
  • Blood: 4
  • Wild Action! Each Cowboy may use Patch ‘em Up once per game. If a cowboy is adjacent to a wounded cowboy, they may heal their friend. To do this, roll a d6 and half the number, rounding up.

Phase 4: Winning

Who all’s left?

  • If a Varmit or Beast kills all the Cowboys, the V/B player wins.
  • If the V/B successfully escapes the game board, the V/B player wins.
  • If the Cowboys kill the V/B, the Cowboy players win.
  • If Charm State is agreed upon, everyone wins!
